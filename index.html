Donate for Flood at Feni, Bangladesh

In August 2024, severe floods struck Feni district in southeastern Bangladesh, displacing hundreds of thousands and submerging over 100 villages. Triggered by heavy monsoon rains and water releases from upstream in India, the floods caused widespread damage to homes, infrastructure, and agriculture. Over 350,000 people were left without power, and critical rescue operations were launched to assist those stranded. Efforts to manage the disaster continue, with extensive damage reported to fisheries and farmlands in the region

       

Donate for Injured Students Participated in Quota Movement BD

The 2024 Bangladesh quota reform movement reignited over discontent with the country's civil service quota system, particularly the 30% reservation for descendants of independence war veterans. Protests, led primarily by students and young professionals, called for reducing quota percentages, prioritizing merit in job recruitment, and ensuring transparency. These protests intensified following a court ruling to reinstate the quota, which had been abolished after similar protests in 2018. The movement sparked widespread demonstrations, internet shutdowns, and mass arrests, as well as confrontations with security forces
